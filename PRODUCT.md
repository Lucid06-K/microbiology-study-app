# Product

## Register
product

## Users
University student(s) studying microbiology — primary user is the app owner, a uni student working through a 13-lecture course (transcripts, slides, MCQs, short-answer, flashcards, mock exams). Used solo, on laptop and phone, in dorm/library/bed across the day. Sessions range from 5-min flashcard reviews to multi-hour exam-prep grinds. The user is here to learn and retain, not to be entertained.

## Product Purpose
A long-form study tool that turns lecture material into durable knowledge. Success = the user opens it daily, completes intended sessions without friction, and feels the app is on their side rather than in the way. Reading, reviewing, and self-testing are the three core jobs; everything else is scaffolding.

## Brand Personality
Scholarly, calm, precise. The voice of a patient TA, not a coach or a cheerleader. Quiet confidence over enthusiasm. Earned warmth (soft neutrals, considered typography), never sugary. No streaks, no celebration confetti, no "great job!". Encouragement is implicit through clarity.

## Anti-references
- Corporate SaaS dashboards (Salesforce, generic admin templates) — busy, chrome-heavy, opinionated for the wrong audience.
- Edgy/dark crypto or developer-tool aesthetics (neon-on-black, aggressive gradients).
- Childlike or gamified study apps (Duolingo, Kahoot, Quizizz) — mascots, streak fire, points, owl personalities.
- Anki — functional but visually punishing; we want the same rigor with humane typography and breathing room.
- Modal-heavy, notification-spamming productivity apps.

## Design Principles
1. **Content is the chrome.** Lecture text, questions, and flashcard prompts are the loudest things on screen. UI sits behind glass, not in front of it.
2. **Quiet by default, expressive on demand.** Restrained color and motion baseline. Reserve emphasis (one accent, a single transition) for moments that matter — a correct answer, a completed session, a mastered card.
3. **Respect the long session.** Type, color, and rhythm must hold up over a two-hour pomodoro. Optimize for reading endurance, not first-impression dazzle.
4. **One thing per screen.** A study session is reading, or recalling, or testing — not a dashboard of all three at once. Surfaces commit to a primary job.
5. **Earned hierarchy.** Scale and weight do the work that color and boxes try to do in SaaS UIs. Cards only when truly the best affordance.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG AA, sensible defaults. Auto light/dark with explicit override. Reduced-motion respect, focus rings on all interactive elements, body type ≥ 16px, line length capped 65–75ch, sufficient touch targets on mobile (44px min).
