Lecture 04 · Bacterial genetics

Horizontal gene transfer reshapes microbial populations.

Three mechanisms, one outcome

Transformation, transduction, and conjugation each move DNA between cells without parent-to-offspring inheritance. The result is the same: a recipient cell that now carries genetic material it did not possess before, and which can be expressed, integrated, or lost over the next several generations.

Lecture 04 · Section 2

Conjugation and the F-plasmid

~6 min read · last opened yesterday
Read Notes MCQ Short answer

Conjugation requires direct contact between a donor and a recipient cell. The donor carries a fertility factor, the F-plasmid, which encodes the machinery for the conjugation pilus.

When the pilus retracts, it draws the two cells into close proximity. A mating bridge forms, and a single strand of plasmid DNA is transferred to the recipient, where complementary synthesis restores the double helix.
